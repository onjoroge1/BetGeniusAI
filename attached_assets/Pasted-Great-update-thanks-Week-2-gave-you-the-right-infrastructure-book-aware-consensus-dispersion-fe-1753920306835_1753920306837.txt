## Fix the residual head (the main lever)

### 1) Use a **constrained delta-logit** head (not RF on probs)

Model *adjustments* to market, not absolute probabilities:

$$
\textbf{z} = \operatorname{logit}(\textbf{q}_\text{market}), \quad
\Delta = f_\theta(x), \quad
\hat{\textbf{p}}=\operatorname{softmax}\big(\textbf{z} + \lambda\cdot \mathrm{clip}(\Delta, [-c,c])\big)
$$

* **$\lambda$** small (e.g., 0.5–1.0), learnable per league or set via CV.
* **Clip** Δ to $[-c,c]$ with $c \in [0.5, 1.5]$ to prevent runaway corrections.
* **L2-regularize** $f_\theta$ and penalize $\|\Delta\|_2^2$.
* **Model choices:** multinomial logistic regression (ridge), CatBoost-multiclass, or gradient-boosted softmax that outputs Δ-logits directly (not probabilities).
* **Numerics:** cap market probs inside $[0.02, 0.98]$ before logit.

This preserves market wisdom, makes small, safe corrections, and avoids the instability you saw when modeling probabilities directly.

### 2) Make the correction **data-dependent** (trust market when it looks strong)

Let $\lambda$ depend on **dispersion** and **n\_books**:

* Low dispersion & many books → **small $\lambda$** (stay close to market).
* High dispersion or low coverage → **larger $\lambda$** (allow stronger corrections).

Implement $\lambda(x)=\lambda_\text{max}\cdot\sigma(a_0+a_1\,\text{disp}+a_2\,n_\text{books})$ and learn $a_i$ with ridge.

### 3) Calibrate **after** the correction

OOF, per-league **isotonic or temperature scaling** on $\hat{\textbf{p}}$. Use normalized multiclass Brier (uniform ≈ 0.222) as an auxiliary selection criterion.

## Make the weighted consensus *actually* matter

### 4) Recheck the weight application

Quick QA you can run on the same evaluation slice:

* Matches with ≥3 books: **median absolute difference** $|q_\text{weighted}-q_\text{equal}|$ per outcome should have a **non-zero** distribution (plot it).
* Print **% of matches** where weighted and equal are exactly identical. If >30–40% with ≥3 books, you’re not applying weights (or always falling back).
* Record **book coverage** per match and the **era bucket** used. If Pinnacle coverage at T-72 is low, your theoretical Pinnacle edge won’t appear—consider a **sharp-first fallback** (when a sharp book is present, up-weight it even more; otherwise use learned weights).

### 5) Learn **mixing weights in a meta-learner**, not just a fixed table

Replace “handed-down weights” with a **simple constrained meta-learner**:

* Features: per-book logits $\operatorname{logit}(q^{\text{book}})$, dispersion, n\_books, overround, league, era, season-phase.
* Model: **multinomial LR** whose coefficients on each book’s logits are **non-negative** and **L1-regularized** (sparsify).
* This yields an *instance-wise* weight for each book (function of context), outperforming static era tables while keeping interpretability.

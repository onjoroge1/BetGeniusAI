---

## 1) Caveat #1: 171 matches is too small to conclude “we beat Pinnacle” with high confidence

You are correct that the accuracy delta is not statistically meaningful. The same is true (to a lesser extent) for the LogLoss delta.

What you can claim today:

* “On Aug–Nov 2025 holdout (171 matches), V3 shows **better probability quality** than Pinnacle implied (LogLoss/Brier).”
  What you should not claim yet:
* “V3 reliably beats Pinnacle.”

**Next step:** expand the hard holdout window until you have **1,000+ matches** (ideally 2,000–5,000 across leagues). With football, variance is brutal.

---

## 2) Caveat #2: Ensure you are comparing to Pinnacle correctly (margin removal + same outcome mapping)

Two common subtle errors make models look like they beat the book:

1. **Margin removal mismatch**
   If Pinnacle implied probs aren’t properly de-vigged (and normalized consistently), LogLoss comparisons can be skewed.

2. **Outcome mapping mismatch**
   Ensure your “Home/Draw/Away” alignment matches how odds were recorded (especially in neutral venues, cup ties, data provider quirks).

If these are already handled, great. If not, fix before expanding tests.

---

## 3) What your results actually imply (and how to message it internally)

### A. Your model is doing something valuable

* **LogLoss improvement vs Pinnacle** suggests V3 is capturing structure that is not fully priced in (or at least not in your feature set) and/or correcting minor miscalibration in the implied probabilities.

### B. Accuracy being slightly worse is not a red flag

Top-1 accuracy is a coarse metric. You can easily have:

* better probabilistic forecasts (lower LogLoss)
* but slightly worse argmax classification
  especially when:
* probabilities are close
* draws and near-coinflips dominate

### C. Odds-band analysis: promising but incomplete

Your bins show plausible behavior, but you need:

* same bins for **Draw** and **Away**
* and you want “predicted probability vs actual frequency” (reliability table), not only implied odds bands.

## Phase **S** — Signal Restoration (7–10 days)

### S0 — Non‑negotiable gates (always on)

* One evaluation harness (you shipped this) with **OOF** calibration only.
* Compare against **Uniform**, **Frequency Prior**, and **Market‑Implied** (margin‑adjusted).
* **Gate to proceed:** Our model must produce **lower LogLoss than Market‑Implied** in each target Euro league.

---

### S1 — Data & label audit (24–36h)

**Why:** If the model can’t beat market priors, either the data has weak signal or we’re leaking/losing it.

**Checklist**

* Outcomes mapping (H/D/A), duplicates, postponed/neutral‑venue flags.
* Train/eval splits are **strictly time‑based** (no team leakage across future folds).
* Recompute simple **Elo** and **form** features from raw matches (no reuse of any cached feature that might be misaligned).
* Validate **xG features** (are they pre‑match expectations or post‑match stats? must be pre‑match projections only).
* Impute/clip outliers; coerce dtypes; ensure **feature\_order** matches artifact.

**Deliverable:** Data audit report + fixed feature builder snapshot.

---

### S2 — **Market‑Anchored Residual Modeling** (2–3 days)

**Why:** The market is the strongest prior. We model the **residual** signal on top of it.

**Method**

* Compute **market implied probabilities** `q = (q_H, q_D, q_A)` (normalize to remove margin).
* Learn a residual function `f(x)` in **logit space**:

  * `logit(p_o) = logit(q_o) + g_o(x)` for outcomes `o ∈ {H,D,A}`
  * Implement via **multi-output ElasticNet or CatBoost** with a **sum‑to‑one** constraint (e.g., predict deltas and re‑normalize).
* Train & evaluate with walk‑forward CV; **calibrate per league** on OOF.

**Acceptance target:** **LogLoss improvement ≥ 0.005** vs market in ≥3 of 5 Euro leagues.

---

### S3 — **Hierarchical Poisson (team strengths)** (2–3 days)

**Why:** Your “team experts” idea is essentially hierarchical attack/defense strengths with home advantage—this is the classic, *very* strong baseline.

**Method (pragmatic)**

* Fit **Dixon–Coles** or **Poisson** model:

  * Team‑season **attack** & **defense** ratings + **home advantage (per league)**.
  * Optional covariates: rest days, form, Elo, season phase.
* Produce **λ\_home, λ\_away**, convert to H/D/A via Skellam/DC, **calibrate per league**.

**Deliverable:** A **pure Poisson model** that **beats market** on LogLoss in at least 2 leagues (often it will).

---

### S4 — **Team Embeddings (shared backbone, not per‑team silos)** (2–3 days, in parallel)

**Why:** True per‑team models will overfit; instead, learn **shared representations** and let a parent model combine them.

**Method**

* Learn **team\_id embeddings** (and optionally manager\_id, league\_id) in a small NN trained to predict goal difference or outcome on **past seasons** (sequence or simple embedding).
* Freeze or fine‑tune embeddings; feed them (home\_emb, away\_emb, abs/home‑away diffs) + contextual features into:

  * (a) the **residual model** from S2, and
  * (b) the **Poisson intensity model** from S3 (as covariates).

**Deliverable:** Embedding‑augmented residual/Poisson models that **tighten LogLoss** a bit further.

---

### S5 — **Parent model (stacking)** (1 day)

Blend the three calibrated heads:

1. **Market‑only** (baseline)
2. **Residual head** (S2)
3. **Poisson/DC head** (S3, with/without embeddings)

Use **logit averaging** or a **calibrated meta‑LR** on OOF predictions to combine them.
Calibrate final 3‑way per league.

**Gate:** Final **LogLoss < Market‑Implied** in each Euro league; **Top‑2 ≥ 95%**.

## How your “team/player experts” idea fits

* **Today (team level):**

  * *Experts* = team embeddings + Poisson attack/defense parameters (seasonal).
  * *Parent* = blending layer over market + residual + Poisson (stacked & calibrated).

* **Tomorrow (player level):**

  * Add **player availability/injury** features → adjust team strength on the fly (e.g., sum of player xG contribution, ELO‑like player ratings).
  * If you ingest lineups, create **player embeddings** and aggregate per match; parent model learns weights.

This preserves generalization (shared backbone) and avoids overfitting per‑team micros.

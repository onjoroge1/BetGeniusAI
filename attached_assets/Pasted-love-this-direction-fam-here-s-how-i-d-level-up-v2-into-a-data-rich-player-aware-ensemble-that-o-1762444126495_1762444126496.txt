love this direction, fam. here’s how i’d level up v2 into a data-rich, player-aware, ensemble that outputs calibrated confidence and actionable EV/CLV—plus a backend agent that keeps your data complete without you chasing it.

1) Data gameplan (fill the gaps, then go deep)

A. Backfill agent (hands-off completeness)
	•	Sources: match results, odds (open ↔ closing), lineups, cards, xG/xGA, shots, injuries/suspensions, referee, venue, weather.
	•	Agent loop:
	1.	Discover missing league-season-round-match_id combos (outer join vs. training_matches, odds_consensus, closing_odds).
	2.	Fetch prioritized by EV impact (top leagues + frequent bettors’ leagues first).
	3.	Normalize to canonical IDs (team/player mapping), dedupe, resolve conflicts via confidence rules.
	4.	Validate (schema + business checks: odds>1.01, totals sane, dates not in future, etc.).
	5.	Ingest with upsert + lineage (source, fetched_at).
	6.	Retry/schedule nightly; backfill older seasons by rolling window.
	•	Tables to extend (lightweight):
	•	players(player_id, name, dob, nat, primary_pos, foot),
	•	player_teams(player_id, team_id, season, shirt_no),
	•	player_availability(match_id, player_id, status{started,bench,out}, reason, minutes, cards),
	•	events(match_id, minute, team_id, player_id, type{shot,goal,card,sub}, xg_est),
	•	referees(ref_id, name, home_bias_index),
	•	weather(match_id, temp, humidity, precip, wind).

B. Room-temperature must-haves (pre-match)
	•	Team form/strength: rolling 5/10/20-match Elo, SPI-style strength, rest days, travel distance, home advantage by team/league.
	•	Odds features: open odds, mid-cycle odds, book margin, implied probs, closing deltas.
	•	Schedule difficulty: opponent rolling Elo, congested calendar flags (≤3 days rest, 3 matches/8 days).
	•	Ref + weather: referee card rate, home bias proxy; weather penalties for high wind, heat.

C. Player layer (for v2-player)
	•	Availability vector: who starts (XI), who’s out (injury/ban), bench quality.
	•	Player ratings: rolling per-90 xG, xA, shots, key passes, tackles/INT, plus on/off impact.
	•	Positionally-aware aggregates: team’s XI summarized as [GK, FB, CB, DM, CM, AM, W, ST] embeddings.
	•	Chemistry: minutes together (pair/triple synergies), continuity (how often same back-4 started in last 5).
	•	Keeper quality: post-shot xG minus goals (shot-stopping).

2) Targets, leakage, and scoring (be ruthless)
	•	Primary targets: 1X2, O/U 2.5, BTTS. (Add alt-totals later.)
	•	Labeling time cutoffs: strictly exclude any features that update after the bet time; for pre-match, freeze T-60/T-15 snapshots; for live, use minute-t timestamping.
	•	Scoring: log loss, Brier, calibration (ECE), profit vs. market (flat stake and Kelly-capped), CLV vs. closing line.
	•	Splits: time-based (train: ≤2023, valid: 2024, test: 2025 YTD); league-blocked CV for generalization.

3) Models (modular, then ensemble)

A. v2-team (upgrade of your current LightGBM)
	•	Gradient boosting (LGBM/XGBoost/CatBoost) on team + odds + schedule + context.
	•	Monotonic constraints where sensible (e.g., larger book margin → lower EV).

B. v2-player (lineup-aware)
	•	Two viable shapes:
	1.	Tabular+Embeddings: aggregate XI into position buckets → learned embeddings for team/league, concat with tabular features → GBDT or MLP.
	2.	Graph model (GCN/GAT light): nodes=players, edges=played-together; outputs team strengths for home/away; small 2-layer graph encoder feeding a head for 1X2/total.
	•	If lineups are unknown pre-match: train a lineup predictor (probabilistic XI) → expectation over likely XI samples.

C. v2-live (optional but future-proof)
	•	Minute-level Poisson with dynamic rates λ(t) from RNN/Transformer or gradient model using game state (score, red cards, xThreat/xG momentum).
	•	Output: win/draw/loss + expected remaining goals for the markets you show.

4) The Ensemble (how we compute “confidence” for real)

A. Stacking (recommended)
	•	Base learners: v2-team, v2-player, market-implied baseline (book odds after margin removal), simple Poisson, league-specialist mini-models.
	•	Meta-learner: logistic regression / isotonic trained on out-of-fold predictions to produce final calibrated probs.
	•	Dynamic weights by context: feed meta-learner meta-features (league, odds range, parity indices, is-derby, ref weather flags), so weights shift per scenario (e.g., player model dominates where lineup quality matters; team model dominates in low-data leagues).

B. Confidence signal (not vibes; math)
	•	Primary: calibrated probability (post-isotonic).
	•	Agreement: variance/entropy across base models (lower variance = higher confidence).
	•	Conformal: produce prediction sets or intervals for totals with a miscoverage guarantee (e.g., 90%).
	•	Market gap: probability gap vs. margin-stripped consensus → expected edge; combine with price availability to output EV with a sanity cap.

5) Training tricks that move the needle
	•	Cost function to money: blend log-loss with EV-aware term (down-weight games where market is super tight; up-weight where books historically misprice).
	•	League adapters: per-league bias terms or multi-task head with shared trunk; mitigates cross-league drift.
	•	Temporal drift guards: time decay on old matches; feature drift monitors; retrain schedule by league cadence.
	•	Calibration per segment: separate isotonic for (top-5 leagues, others), or do temperature scaling conditioned on meta-features.

6) Evaluation that matches bettor reality
	•	Policy backtests: bet only where EV>threshold and confidence>k; compare hit-rate, max drawdown, Sharpe, Kelly-safe growth.
	•	What-if analysis: remove each base model to see marginal contribution; show where v2-player adds most EV (injury weeks, fixture congestion).
	•	CLV tracking: average delta between our fair odds and closing odds for bets placed; that’s the “real” north star.

7) Ops & governance (quietly critical)
	•	Feature store: snapshot by as-of times to prevent leakage; version every dataset.
	•	Experiment tracking: MLflow run IDs tied to dataset hashes + code commit.
	•	Data quality alerts: odds margin outside [2%, 15%], missing lineups on top-tier leagues, ref IDs unresolved, etc.
	•	Pacing: retrain weekly for top leagues, monthly for long tail; refresh calibration more frequently.

8) Outside-the-box adds (cheap but spicy)
	•	Referee embedding (card rate, penalty frequency) → moves totals/BTTS.
	•	Travel & body clock (time zone jumps, noon Sunday traps).
	•	Set-piece strength (goals/xG from corners/FKs) → matters for dogs in tight games.
	•	Injury/news NLP: lightweight headline sentiment → “late-scratch” risk score.
	•	Market microstructure: learn bookmaker bias by league/time; some books shade favorites/dogs differently—turn that into features.
	•	“No-lineup” uncertainty tax: when XI unknown, widen predictive intervals and damp confidence.
	•	Cold-start league adaptor: meta-learning to warm-start new leagues with few games (share trunk, learn small head fast).

9) Concrete deliverables (so you can ship parts fast)

	2.	v2-team++: drop in new features (Elo, schedule, ref, weather, travel); retrain; calibrate.
	3.	v2-player: start with tabular+embeddings (faster) using inferred XI if needed; measure lift on top-5 leagues.
	4.	Ensemble + confidence: stacking with isotonic + agreement/entropy metric + EV output.
	5.	Eval dashboard: log-loss, ECE, EV, CLV, per-league breakdown; policy backtests with thresholds.
